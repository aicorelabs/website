COMMON QUESTIONS YOU MIGHT HAVE 
Do you have any of these concerns? We have some answers here for you
Q1: I'm not technical at all. Can I still work with Zeffron?
Yes — and frankly, most of our best engagements are with non-technical founders. You do not need to understand code, architecture, or infrastructure to work with us. What you need is a clear problem, a target user, and a willingness to make decisions fast. We handle everything else and we will explain the technical parts in plain language, flag the trade-offs that matter to your business, and make sure you feel in control throughout the build.
Q2  How is Zeffron different from hiring a freelancer or a larger agency?
Freelancers are affordable but solo. They build what you ask for, and if you ask for the wrong thing, they build that too. Larger agencies are thorough but slow, you will wait months for a kickoff, pay for project managers, and still not own the product thinking. Zeffron sits in neither category. We are a lean senior team that moves at startup speed. We will tell you when a feature is a distraction, when a simpler architecture is the smarter bet, and when you are ready to raise before you think you are.
Q3  What if I need changes after the build days are up?
The first week after launch is included in every sprint,  if something is not right, we fix it. Beyond that, most founders who want to keep building move onto a monthly retainer with us, where we act as their ongoing technical team. Some projects roll directly from a sprint into a Custom AI build or a fractional team arrangement depending on what growth looks like. We will always be transparent about what makes sense for your stage before recommending anything.
Q4  I have an idea but I am not sure it is ready. How developed does my concept need to be before we talk?
Not very. What matters is not the polish of your brief,  it is whether the problem you are solving is real. If you can describe who your user is, what they are struggling with today, and roughly what you imagine the solution looks like, that is enough to start. Our discovery process is specifically designed to turn loose thinking into a sharp, scoped product plan.
Q5  Do I own the code and the intellectual property after the build?
Completely and contractually. Everything we build for you belongs to you: the code, the design, the data architecture, all of it. We do not retain licensing rights, we do not keep back-end access after handover, and we do not use your product as a portfolio asset without your explicit permission. 
Q6  Can you help me get investor-ready — not just build the product?
Yes. Investor readiness is one of our four core services for exactly this reason. We have seen too many founders walk into fundraising conversations with a strong product and a weak story — or worse, a strong story and a product that falls apart under technical scrutiny. Our Investor and Pitch Readiness package includes a live product demo, technical documentation for due diligence, a co-developed pitch deck, and a GTM plan. We can also join investor calls to answer technical questions directly if that would be useful. Getting funded is not just about having a good product. It is about being able to show it clearly, defend it confidently, and frame it in a language investors already trust.
Q7: I already have something built  but it was put together quickly and I am not sure it is production-ready. Can you help?
Yes, and this is one of the most common situations we work with. A lot of founders today are using AI-assisted tools to ship a first version fast — which is smart for validating an idea, but those builds were not designed to scale, handle real user traffic, or pass a security review. What you have is a starting point, not a finished product. We come in, audit what exists, identify where it breaks under pressure, fix the underlying architecture, and harden it for production. This includes security vulnerabilities, data handling, performance under load, and making the codebase something a future engineering team can actually work with. You keep the momentum you have built. We make it solid enough to put in front of real users, enterprise clients, or investors without it becoming a liability.
