# Zeffron homepage moodboards

Purpose: six controlled homepage variants for A/B testing. Each keeps the Zeffron logo, brand blue, regulated-team positioning, method, platform credibility, and consultation CTA. Five routes commit to one industry vertical; the sixth tests Zeffron as a SWE + applied AI delivery team.

## Research base

- GOV.UK Government Design Principles: start with user needs, do less, design with data, make complex things simple, design for everyone.
- WCAG 2.2: keep contrast, focus, structure, and understandable content visible across variants.
- Carbon and enterprise-grid design practice: use grid discipline, spacing rhythm, and consistent component patterns for B2B trust.
- Current 2026 visual trend reading: move away from generic synthetic AI gloss toward tactility, texture, and distinct human signal.

## Variant 1: Trust Ledger

Mapped vertical: Legal operations.

Mood: Swiss governance, procurement confidence, service-design restraint.

Page architecture: ledger-style audit sheet. The page leads with legal operational drag, buyer signal, Zeffron response, mapped vertical, legal workflow stack, and method.

Workflow logos: Clio, DocuSign, Microsoft 365, SharePoint, Google Drive, Slack, OpenAI, LangChain.

Design cues:
- Off-black canvas, ivory type, Zeffron blue as the only strong accent.
- Ledger-like rows, sharp edges, controlled proof statements.
- Best for buyers who need risk, compliance, and measured deployment to be clear immediately.

Route: `/home/trust-ledger`

## Variant 2: Clinical Calm

Mapped vertical: Healthcare.

Mood: clean healthcare trust, generous light space, measured and low-friction.

Page architecture: reassurance pathway. The page leads with healthcare sensitivity, problem/impact/solution, mapped vertical, then a four-step deployment path before the healthcare workflow stack.

Workflow logos: Epic, Oracle Health, HL7 FHIR, Microsoft Azure, Salesforce Health Cloud, Zoom, DocuSign, OpenAI.

Design cues:
- Light clinical surface, blue accents, calm rhythm, high readability.
- Softer visual treatment without becoming consumer wellness.
- Best for healthcare and sensitive-service buyers who need trust before technical depth.

Route: `/home/clinical-calm`

## Variant 3: Executive Editorial

Mapped vertical: Financial services.

Mood: boardroom editorial, cinematic confidence, premium consulting authority.

Page architecture: executive thesis. The page opens with a large financial-services argument, then moves through cost, control, proof, research, and financial workflow stack.

Workflow logos: Salesforce, Stripe, Plaid, Xero, QuickBooks, Snowflake, Databricks, OpenAI.

Design cues:
- High-contrast typography, financial-services imagery, restrained luxury.
- Less dashboard, more senior advisory.
- Best for founder, partner, or executive audiences comparing consultancies.

Route: `/home/executive-editorial`

## Variant 4: Operator Console

Mapped vertical: Manufacturing.

Mood: technical control room, stack credibility, dense but composed operational signal.

Page architecture: technical console. The page moves manufacturing workflow stack and release posture immediately below the hero, then shows workflow signals, production method, and mapped vertical.

Workflow logos: SAP, Siemens, Autodesk, Dynamics 365, Oracle, Snowflake, Databricks, Power BI.

Design cues:
- Dark operational grid, high contrast, stronger technical proof.
- Stack logos and evaluation language feel native rather than decorative.
- Best for technical buyers and AI-literate operations teams.

Route: `/home/operator-console`

## Variant 5: Human Service

Mapped vertical: Ecommerce and retail.

Mood: warm professional service, tactile photography, practical language for non-technical buyers.

Page architecture: staff-relief journey. The page follows before/during/after ecommerce service pressure, then mapped vertical, ecommerce workflow stack, and delivery method.

Workflow logos: Shopify, WhatsApp, Gmail, Google Sheets, Klaviyo, Stripe, Zendesk, Meta Ads.

Design cues:
- Human operations photography, warm dark palette, plain-language problem framing.
- Less technical intimidation, more service relationship and staff relief.
- Best for non-technical buyers who feel admin pain before AI interest.

Route: `/home/human-service`

## Variant 6: Applied AI Engineering

Mapped service line: SWE + Applied AI.

Mood: product engineering, applied AI systems, technical buyer confidence, startup-to-production pace.

Page architecture: production engineering page. The page leads with the gap between demo and shippable product, then shows mobile, web, and multi-agent systems as production software surfaces, the SWE + AI delivery stack, prototype-hardening signals, and method.

Workflow logos: React Native, Expo, Swift, Kotlin, Next.js, Vercel, Supabase, PostgreSQL, GitHub, OpenAI, LangGraph, Anthropic.

Design cues:
- Dark engineering console with Zeffron blue as a precise systems accent.
- More product-team language, less sector consulting language.
- Copy should create buyer tension around prototype-to-production risk, not merely list engineering capabilities.
- Best for founders, product leaders, and technical teams who need software engineering plus applied AI delivery.

Route: `/home/applied-ai-engineering`
